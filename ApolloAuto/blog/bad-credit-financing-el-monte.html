What lenders look at beyond your credit score

In the San Gabriel Valley used car market, lenders consider the full picture — not just the number:

  • Steady income. Proof of income (pay stubs or bank statements) is often more important than your score. Lenders want to know you can make the payment.
  • Down payment. Even a modest amount down improves approval odds and reduces your monthly payment.
  • Employment stability. Six months or more at the same employer signals lower risk to lenders.
  • Time since negative events. A bankruptcy or repossession from a few years ago matters less than one from last year. Recovery is factored in.
  • Trade-in equity. If you have a vehicle to trade, it reduces the amount you need to finance — improving your position.

How to prepare for your El Monte visit

  1. Know your real monthly budget. Include payment, insurance, gas, and maintenance — not just the car price.
  2. Bring proof of income. Recent pay stubs or 2–3 months of bank statements work. Self-employed buyers can use bank statements.
  3. Bring a valid ID. California driver's license or state ID.
  4. Think about your trade-in. Even a rough estimate of your current vehicle's value helps Tim find the right package before you sit down.
  5. Be honest about your situation. Tim has helped buyers with every kind of credit history. The more he knows, the better the options he can offer.
